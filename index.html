The Challenge

The AI Confidence Gap in Radiology

Your hospital has invested in AI diagnostic tools. They're powerful. But every new tool introduces risk: Will it work in your environment? Can you trust it with your patients? How do you validate before deployment?

AI tools vary by environment

US- or EU-trained models frequently underperform on APAC patient populations, local equipment protocols, and regional imaging standards. Lab accuracy ≠ clinical accuracy.

Delayed AI adoption

Hospitals carry all the risk

Vendors provide accuracy claims. Regulators require validation. Radiologists need assurance. There is no independent infrastructure to bridge that gap — until now.

Liability exposure

Radiologists need backup

Clinicians are being asked to trust AI they cannot independently verify. Without a rigorous validation layer, radiologist confidence in AI tools — and patient safety — is at risk.

The Solution

Process

Three Steps to Confident AI Deployment

A structured, repeatable process that delivers validation evidence in weeks — not the months of internal studies.

1

Integration & Scoping

You provide anonymized imaging datasets from your environment. We integrate your chosen AI tool into our validation platform. We map your imaging protocols, PACS/RIS infrastructure, and clinical workflows. No disruption to live clinical operations.

2

Blinded Validation Study

We run a rigorous, blinded clinical validation study using your data, your patient demographics, your imaging protocols. Your radiologists review findings independently. Performance metrics emerge from real-world conditions — not lab data.

3

Regulatory Evidence Package

You receive a comprehensive clinical validation report: performance summaries, confidence intervals, safety assessments, edge case analysis, and failure mode documentation. Built to satisfy FDA, CE, TGA, and HKHA standards.

Result: You deploy verified AI tools with confidence. Your radiologists know exactly how the tool performs in your specific environment — before it touches a patient.
